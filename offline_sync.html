Offline Synchronization Conflict Resolution

Priority-based business rules for handling conflicts when field technicians sync offline work with online system updates

The Challenge: Offline capability is critical for field technicians in areas with poor connectivity. However, conflict resolution is complex: What happens when a technician updates a task offline while a PM re-assigns it online? Without defined business rules, developers make arbitrary technical decisions that may not align with business needs, leading to data loss and user distrust.
P1
Highest
Safety & Compliance (Always Wins)
Rule: Any HSE incident report or safety-related data entered offline ALWAYS syncs and is never overwritten.
Rationale: Safety data is legally required and time-sensitive; losing it is unacceptable. Example: If technician reports a safety incident offline, it syncs even if the work order was cancelled online.
P2
High
Field Work Completion (Wins Unless Contradicted by Admin)
Rule: Field technician's work completion data (status updates, photos, time logs) syncs successfully UNLESS an admin/PM has explicitly cancelled or re-assigned the task online.
Conflict Handling: If task was re-assigned online → Offline work is saved but flagged for PM review with notification: "Technician A completed this task offline, but you re-assigned it to Technician B. Please review." Field work is the core value-add; we don't want to lose completed work, but PM decisions override.
P3
Medium
Inventory Transactions (Validate Against Current State)
Rule: Offline material releases sync only if current inventory level is sufficient; otherwise, flagged for warehouse review.
Conflict Handling: If offline release of 50 units, but current inventory is only 30 units → Transaction is saved as "Pending Approval" and warehouse staff is notified to review and approve/reject. Prevents negative inventory and material shortages, but doesn't lose transaction record.
P4
Standard
Administrative Changes (Admin Wins, Notify User)
Rule: If PM or admin makes a change online (re-assign, cancel, edit) while technician is working offline, the admin change takes precedence.
Conflict Handling: Technician's offline work is saved in "Conflict History" log. Technician receives notification upon sync: "This task was re-assigned while you were offline. Your work has been saved for review." PM can view conflict history and decide whether to merge or discard offline work. Admin/PM decisions are strategic and should not be overwritten by field-level actions.
