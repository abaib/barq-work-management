Comprehensive Change Management Plan

Ensuring smooth transition for 250+ users from manual processes to digital ERP through structured adoption program

The Challenge: Moving 250+ users across 8 different roles from manual, paper-based processes to a comprehensive digital ERP represents a significant cultural shift. Current plan's 1-2 week training window is insufficient and risks low adoption, user resistance, and failure to achieve promised efficiency gains.
Pillar 1: Pre-Launch Communication
Weeks 1-6
"What's Coming and Why" messaging campaign for all 250+ users
  • Executive sponsorship: Leadership video explaining business case
  • Role-specific benefit messaging: "What's in it for me?"
  • Monthly town halls and Q&A sessions
  • Visual roadmap showing timeline and expectations
  • Success stories from pilot users or similar implementations
Pillar 2: Super-User Champion Program
Weeks 5-8
Identify 15-20 "champions" from each user group as peer support
  • 3-4 Field Technicians (respected by peers, tech-savvy)
  • 2-3 Project Managers (influential, early adopters)
  • 2-3 Warehouse Staff (experienced, process-oriented)
  • Early access to system in Week 5 (UAT phase)
  • Intensive 2-day training workshop (Week 6)
  • Recognition program: Certificates, badges, executive acknowledgment
Pillar 3: Role-Specific Training
Weeks 7-8
Scenario-based training beyond generic "click here" instructions
  • Field Technicians: "Complete a WO from start to finish with photos"
  • Project Managers: "Create WO from screenshot, assign, track"
  • Warehouse Staff: "Process material release and track inventory"
  • Hands-on practice in training environment with realistic data
  • Training materials in both Arabic and English
  • Video tutorials (3-5 minutes each) for on-demand reference
Pillar 4: Post-Launch Hyper-Care
Weeks 9-12
Dedicated support period for 2-4 weeks after full deployment
  • On-site support team at main office and key field sites
  • Rapid response help desk: <2 hour response time for critical issues
  • Daily stand-up meetings with super-users to identify issues
  • Weekly feedback sessions with user groups
  • Issue tracking dashboard: Categorize and prioritize problems
  • Quick-win improvements: Deploy small fixes based on feedback
